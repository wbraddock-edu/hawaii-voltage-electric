Signs Your Electrical Panel Needs an Upgrade

📅 April 2025✍ Rafael Aquino⏱ 7 min read

Your electrical panel is the heart of your home's electrical system. On Maui, where older housing stock is common and electrical loads have grown dramatically with EV chargers, solar systems, and modern appliances, panel upgrades have become one of our most requested services. Here are the signs that your panel may need attention.

Frequent Tripped Breakers

Breakers trip occasionally — that's what they're designed to do when a circuit is overloaded. But if you're regularly resetting breakers, especially on specific circuits, it indicates those circuits are consistently being pushed to their limits. This might mean you need dedicated circuits for high-draw appliances, or your panel simply doesn't have the capacity for your current electrical usage.

Panel Age: 25+ Years

Electrical panels from the 1970s through 1990s are reaching the end of their design life. Two specific brands — Federal Pacific Electric and Zinsco — have been identified as fire hazards due to breaker design defects. If your home has either of these, replacement should be a priority regardless of age. We identify the panel brand during any service visit.

You're Adding Significant New Loads

EV chargers, solar systems with battery backup, heat pump water heaters, and whole-home generators all require substantial electrical capacity. Before installing any of these, we assess whether your existing panel can handle the additional load. In many cases, upgrading to a 200-amp panel at the same time as a new load installation is the most cost-effective approach.

You Have a Fuse Box

Some older Maui homes still have fuse-based electrical service. These are significantly outdated for modern electrical loads and present insurance complications — many insurers will not cover homes with fuse panels or will charge significantly higher premiums. Replacement with a modern circuit breaker panel is straightforward and typically cost-effective.
