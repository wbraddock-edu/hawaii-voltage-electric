Our emergency response target is under 2 hours for Wailuku and central Maui. Other areas of Maui typically within 2–4 hours. We charge a $150 emergency dispatch fee plus labor for after-hours calls. For life-safety emergencies involving electrical fires or live wires, call 911 first.
A panel upgrade typically involves: permitting with the county, coordination with HECO (Hawaiian Electric) for meter and service entrance work, installation of the new panel with appropriate breakers, arc fault and GFCI protection as required by current code, and a final inspection. Most residential panel upgrades take one full day. HECO service connection work adds 1–2 weeks in scheduling.

Signs you may need a panel upgrade: breakers that trip frequently, a panel older than 25 years (especially Federal Pacific or Zinsco brands — fire hazards), you're adding an EV charger or solar, you're adding significant new loads (hot tub, large AC, workshop), or you still have a fuse panel. We'll assess your panel during any service visit and give you an honest recommendation.
