Hawaii Energy Costs and How to Reduce Them

📅 March 2025✍ Rafael Aquino⏱ 7 min read

Hawaii consistently ranks as the most expensive state for electricity in the United States. Maui residents typically pay between 38 and 45 cents per kilowatt-hour — more than three times the national average of around 13 cents. For a typical Maui household using 600 kWh per month, that's a monthly electric bill approaching $250–$270. Understanding where that energy goes and how to reduce it is more financially meaningful in Hawaii than almost anywhere else in the country.

The Biggest Energy Consumers

In a typical Hawaii home, air conditioning accounts for 45–55% of energy use during warmer months. Water heating is typically 15–20%. Lighting, refrigeration, and entertainment electronics make up most of the rest. Understanding this breakdown helps prioritize where to invest in efficiency improvements.

Solar: The Most Impactful Investment

With rates above 40 cents per kWh, solar panels in Hawaii have some of the shortest payback periods in the country — typically 5–8 years for a well-sized system. After payback, the electricity is essentially free for the remaining 20–25 year panel life. Voltage Electric handles all the electrical work for solar installation, from inverter wiring to HECO interconnection.

EV Charging Strategy

If you drive an EV, charging time-of-use rates are important. HECO's demand response and time-of-use programs allow you to charge at lower rates during off-peak hours (typically late night to early morning). A smart charger with scheduling capabilities can automatically charge during the cheapest hours. We can install and configure these systems during EV charger installation.

LED Lighting Retrofit

LED lighting uses 75–80% less electricity than incandescent bulbs and lasts 25 times longer. A comprehensive LED retrofit of a typical Maui home can reduce lighting energy costs by $15–$30 per month. Our lighting upgrade service handles full commercial and residential retrofits, including recessed lighting conversions and exterior security lighting.
