Generator Installation for Hurricane Prep in Hawaii

📅 February 2025✍ Rafael Aquino⏱ 7 min read

The 2023 Maui wildfires were a stark reminder that Hawaii's island infrastructure — power lines, fuel supply chains, water systems — is uniquely vulnerable. Even setting aside extreme events like wildfires, Maui experiences grid outages from tropical storms, equipment failures, and the challenges of maintaining aging infrastructure across a geographically isolated island. Whole-home standby generators have seen a surge in demand since 2023, and for good reason.

Standby vs Portable Generators

Portable generators cost less upfront but require manual setup, must be operated outdoors due to carbon monoxide risk, and run on fuel that must be stored and replenished. Standby generators are permanently installed, automatically activate within seconds of a power outage, and run on piped natural gas or a large propane tank. For homeowners who want true protection — especially if they rely on medical equipment, have vulnerable household members, or rent their property — standby generators are the superior choice.

Sizing Your Generator

Generator sizing depends on what you want to power during an outage. A 7–10kW generator can power essential circuits: refrigerator, lights, a few outlets, and a window AC unit. A 14–22kW generator can run most of a modern home including a central AC system. We conduct a load calculation during the quoting process to recommend the right size for your specific needs and budget.

The Automatic Transfer Switch

An automatic transfer switch (ATS) is the critical safety device that disconnects your home from the utility grid before the generator powers on. This protects utility workers and your generator from dangerous backfeed situations. Hawaii code requires an ATS for standby generator installations — we handle all permitting and inspection as part of every generator project.

Propane Tank Sizing for Maui

Most Maui homes without natural gas service use propane generators. Tank sizing affects how long you can run without a refill. A 250-gallon tank gives roughly 5–7 days of whole-home generator operation. A 500-gallon tank provides 10–14 days — more than enough for most weather events. We coordinate with local propane suppliers on tank installation as part of the project.
