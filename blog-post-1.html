Solar Panel Wiring Basics for Hawaii Homeowners

📅 June 2025✍ Rafael Aquino⏱ 7 min read

Hawaii leads the nation in rooftop solar adoption, and Maui is no exception. With electricity rates consistently above 40 cents per kilowatt-hour — among the highest in the US — the financial incentive to go solar is compelling. But solar panels are only half the system. The electrical infrastructure that connects them to your home, your grid, and your battery backup is equally important.

How Solar Connects to Your Home

A residential solar system starts with the panels, which generate DC (direct current) electricity. This DC power flows through wiring to an inverter — either a central string inverter or individual microinverters on each panel — which converts it to AC (alternating current) that your home uses. From the inverter, the power connects to your main electrical panel, where it's distributed to your home's circuits.

The Role of the Main Panel

Your main panel is the hub where solar power enters your home system. In most cases, we install a dedicated solar breaker in your existing panel. If your panel is older or lacks capacity, we may need to upgrade it before solar installation. This is especially common in older Maui homes that were built with 100-amp service — not enough for modern loads plus solar production.

Battery Backup Integration

Battery systems like the Tesla Powerwall or Enphase IQ Battery add complexity to the electrical design. A battery backup requires a gateway or automatic transfer switch that isolates your home from the grid during outages, allowing the battery to power your home independently. This is critical for Maui after events like the 2023 wildfires demonstrated the vulnerability of island grid infrastructure.

HECO Interconnection

To feed excess solar power back to the grid (net metering), your system must be interconnected with Hawaiian Electric (HECO). This requires a formal application, technical review, and installation of a bidirectional meter. The electrical work must be completed by a licensed electrical contractor and inspected before HECO will connect your system.
